# Founding Engineer Hiring Process

## Hiring Intent

We are hiring one founding engineer who can build product end-to-end under ambiguity, ship fast, and raise the engineering bar as the team scales.

## Role Definition

### Core Mission

Own critical product and platform delivery for the MVP while partnering directly with leadership on roadmap and execution tradeoffs.

### Must-Have Capabilities

- Build and ship full-stack features with minimal oversight.
- Operate in early-stage ambiguity and convert vague goals into deliverable plans.
- Make pragmatic architecture decisions that preserve speed and reliability.
- Communicate clearly in writing and async collaboration.
- Demonstrate strong product judgment and customer focus.

### Nice-to-Have Capabilities

- Experience mentoring junior engineers.
- Experience with agent tooling, developer platforms, or automation.
- Prior startup or first-10-engineering-hires context.

## Scorecard

Rate each dimension on a 1-4 scale.

### Scale

- 1: Does not meet the bar.
- 2: Mixed signal; below expected level for this role.
- 3: Meets the bar for a founding engineer.
- 4: Exceeds the bar and raises team capability.

### Dimensions and Weights

| Dimension | Weight | What "3" Looks Like |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Technical Depth | 25% | Solves complex engineering problems with sound tradeoffs and clean implementation choices. |
| Execution Velocity | 25% | Delivers meaningful scope quickly without compromising critical quality. |
| Product Judgment | 20% | Connects engineering decisions to user impact and business outcomes. |
| Ownership and Autonomy | 15% | Proactively drives work from problem to shipped result. |
| Collaboration and Communication | 15% | Communicates crisply, aligns stakeholders, and improves team decisions. |

## Interview Loop

### Stage 0: Resume + Portfolio Screen (20 minutes)

Goal: Validate baseline fit and avoid false positives.

- Review evidence of 0-to-1 shipping.
- Confirm direct contribution on key projects.
- Check for role-level mismatch.

Exit criteria:

- Pass: move to recruiter/CEO screen.
- Fail: reject with reason tag.

### Stage 1: CEO Screen (30 minutes)

Goal: Test mission alignment, communication clarity, and startup fit.

Focus areas:

- Motivation for early-stage risk.
- Example of shipping under ambiguity.
- Strength of written and verbal reasoning.

Exit criteria:

- Pass if candidate demonstrates strong ownership and clear motivation.

### Stage 2: Technical Deep Dive (60 minutes)

Goal: Evaluate technical depth and decision quality.

Format:

- Candidate walks through a recent system they built.
- Interviewer probes architecture choices, constraints, failure modes, and tradeoffs.

Exit criteria:

- Pass if reasoning is concrete, pragmatic, and technically rigorous.

### Stage 3: Practical Build Exercise (90 minutes)

Goal: Evaluate execution speed, code quality, and product instincts.

Format:

- Scoped build task representative of our stack and roadmap.
- Candidate can ask clarifying questions and explain tradeoffs.

Assessment:

- Correctness and completeness.
- Prioritization under time pressure.
- Quality of written notes/README and test strategy.

### Stage 4: Collaboration + Values Interview (45 minutes)

Goal: Validate team dynamics and conflict handling.

Focus areas:

- How candidate handles disagreement.
- How they seek and give feedback.
- How they drive cross-functional alignment.

### Stage 5: Final Decision Panel (30 minutes)

Participants: CEO + interviewers.

Process:

- Review scorecard signals before compensation discussion.
- Explicitly call out any weak signal or missing data.
- Decide: hire, no-hire, or additional signal needed.

## Decision Rubric

## Hire Threshold

A candidate is "hire" only when all are true:

- Weighted score >= 3.2/4.0.
- No dimension scored 1 by any interviewer.
- At least two independent strong signals (score 4) in core dimensions.
- Clear evidence they can operate autonomously in a founding environment.

## No-Hire Triggers

- Repeated weak execution signal (velocity or ownership < 3).
- Product judgment < 3 with no evidence of learning agility.
- Collaboration concerns that would lower team throughput.

## Tie-Break Rule

If candidate score is between 2.9 and 3.2:

- Run one targeted follow-up interview for the weakest core dimension.
- Make final decision within 48 hours after follow-up.

## Process Metrics

Track weekly:

- Funnel conversion by stage.
- Time-to-decision.
- Offer acceptance rate.
- False-negative and false-positive post-hire signals.

Use metrics to recalibrate loop strictness and interview prompts monthly.
